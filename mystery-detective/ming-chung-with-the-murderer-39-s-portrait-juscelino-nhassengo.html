A Chinese boy, in the name of Tsai Ming-Chung, lives with his parents until he's six years old. The privilege of growing at his parents' side is frustrated when an assault to the family house ends with the murder of both father and mother. The living routine reserved to his parents turns into a recluse personality. A year and a half later the boy calls the principal's attention with mysterious drawings. The obsession with the same portrait forms the hypothesis that he's manifesting memories of the murderer and the principal calls the police.
